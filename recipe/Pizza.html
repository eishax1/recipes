Pizza

Description

Indulge in the timeless simplicity of a Classic Margherita Pizza, a true testament to the beauty of fresh, high-quality ingredients. This pizza features a thin, crispy crust topped with a vibrant tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella cheese, and fragrant basil leaves. The dough is crafted from a mix of all-purpose and whole wheat flour, providing a perfect balance of chewiness and crunch. Once baked to perfection, the mozzarella melts into a bubbly, golden blanket, while the fresh basil adds a burst of herbal freshness. Drizzled with extra-virgin olive oil and a sprinkle of sea salt, this Margherita Pizza is a celebration of traditional Italian flavors that will transport your taste buds straight to Naples.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (preferably San Marzano tomatoes, crushed)
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, salt, and sugar. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the dry ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough:Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  3. Preheat the oven:Place a pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven and preheat to 475°F (245°C).
  4. Shape the dough:Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a floured surface. Roll or stretch the dough into a 12-inch circle. Transfer the dough to a piece of parchment paper for easy handling.
  5. Assemble the pizza:Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Arrange the mozzarella slices on top of the sauce.
  6. Bake the pizza:Carefully slide the pizza (with the parchment paper) onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes until slightly browned
  7. Serve!
